# 90-Day AI Adoption Authority Plan

## Purpose

This plan turns ikramrana.com from a completed website project into a regularly updated authority source on practical AI adoption in businesses.

The editorial position is:

> AI should earn its place in a business workflow. Start with the work, define the human decisions, and measure the complete result before expanding the technology.

The objective is not to publish generic AI news. The objective is to answer the questions business owners are asking while adding Ikram Rana's critical and practical judgment.

## Audience

Business owners, founders, and decision-makers who know AI may be useful but do not yet have a reliable way to decide:

- which workflow should change first;
- whether AI belongs in that workflow;
- what information the system may access;
- which decisions must remain human;
- how the result should be measured; and
- whether a pilot is ready to become part of normal operations.

## Research foundation

The first cycle is grounded in public, inspectable sources.

1. Statistics Canada reported that 19.2% of businesses used AI to produce goods or deliver services in the 12 months preceding its second-quarter 2026 survey, up from 12.2% in 2025 and 6.1% in 2024.
   - Source: https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/n1/pub/11-621-m/11-621-m2026010-eng.pdf
2. The same survey reported cybersecurity or privacy concerns as the most commonly identified barrier to AI use, followed by cost. It also found that 44.4% of AI-using businesses changed training or staffing practices.
   - Source: https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/n1/pub/11-621-m/11-621-m2026010-eng.pdf
3. Statistics Canada research found that complementary capabilities such as data analytics, cloud computing, employee training, and other business capabilities are associated with AI adoption. This supports an operational-readiness argument rather than a tool-first argument.
   - Source: https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/n1/pub/36-28-0001/2026004/article/00002-eng.htm
4. The Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada advises businesses using generative AI to address legal authority, transparency, explainability, safeguards, limits on sharing personal or confidential information, and privacy by design.
   - Source: https://www.priv.gc.ca/en/privacy-topics/technology/artificial-intelligence/ai_business/
5. The NIST AI Risk Management Framework calls for defined business value, documented human oversight, knowledge limits, expected benefits and costs, testing, monitoring, and controls for third-party AI systems.
   - Source: https://airc.nist.gov/airmf-resources/airmf/5-sec-core/
6. The OECD's 2025 G7 discussion paper describes adoption gaps and identifies connectivity, data, algorithms, compute, skills, and finance as important enablers.
   - Source: https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/ai-adoption-by-small-and-medium-sized-enterprises_426399c1-en.html

These sources support the topic selection. They do not prove Ikram's private client results, Barrana performance claims, or any hypothetical case-study result.

## Publishing system

Each approved topic produces one coordinated content package:

1. A LinkedIn post that introduces the position and invites discussion.
2. A Substack Note that gives one sharp observation or practical test.
3. A LinkedIn article with FAQs that develops the argument and answers search-relevant questions.
4. A website article or a meaningful update to an existing page when the topic deserves a permanent canonical source.

The website is the long-term authority source. Social channels create discovery and send readers back to the relevant canonical page.

## Editorial requirements

Every permanent article must include:

- a business question in plain language;
- a direct answer near the top;
- the workflow problem that exists before AI;
- Ikram's position;
- where AI may help;
- where it may fail or make the work worse;
- human decisions and escalation points;
- a practical first step;
- sources and reviewed date;
- relevant internal links; and
- the CTA: **Find the Workflow AI Should Fix First.**

No em dashes may be used. Technical language should be explained or removed.

## Claim safeguards

- Public statistics must link to the original source and retain the correct date and context.
- Private client work must not be identifiable without authorization.
- Demo case studies and sample figures must be labelled as illustrative scenarios.
- No article may imply that Ikram is licensed to practise law in Ontario.
- Pakistan legal credentials must use the verified jurisdiction and proportionate wording.
- The Singapore government project may only be described with the approved subcontracting context and without implying direct engagement or endorsement.
- PIPEDA-related statements must describe concrete privacy practices and must not promise universal legal compliance without a scoped review.

## Workflow and ownership

1. An idea is added through the GitHub **Website content request** form.
2. The evidence and claim status are checked.
3. A content brief is approved.
4. The website draft and coordinated social package are prepared.
5. Ikram reviews the position, tone, facts, and CTA.
6. The branch passes type checking, production build, link review, and mobile review.
7. The website change is merged through a pull request.
8. The deployment owner publishes the approved build.
9. Search indexing and performance are monitored.
10. The page is updated when evidence, guidance, or the business landscape changes.

## Measurement baseline

Record a baseline after the Phase 2 website is live. Review monthly:

- indexed pages and indexing errors;
- non-branded search queries and clicks;
- queries that combine Ikram Rana with AI adoption or a business problem;
- visits to framework, industry, use-case, and article pages;
- CTA clicks and completed discovery requests;
- referring domains and citations;
- engagement with LinkedIn posts, Substack Notes, and LinkedIn articles;
- which questions lead people to the site; and
- content that needs correction, expansion, consolidation, or retirement.

Do not treat ranking movement or LLM mentions as proof of business impact. Connect attention to qualified conversations and useful reader behaviour.

## Review gates

- Gate 1: Phase 2 is live and the deployment smoke test has passed.
- Gate 2: Search Console and analytics baselines are recorded.
- Gate 3: The first four content briefs are approved.
- Gate 4: The first website article and coordinated social package are reviewed before publication.
- Gate 5: Results are reviewed after 30 days before increasing publishing volume.
